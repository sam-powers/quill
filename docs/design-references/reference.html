The core tension in any platform decision is the gap between what users ask for and what they actually need. We've consistently found that the most durable product choices resist the pull toward feature completeness.

On prioritization

When we look at retention curves, the signal is clear: users who reach the "aha moment" within the first session retain at nearly twice the rate of those who don't.

The proposed framework: must-have, should-have, and nice-to-have critical path, high-leverage, and exploratory. Each tier carries a different resourcing commitment.

Open questions

We haven't resolved how to handle platform requests that are high-effort but strategically important. The current scoring model underweights strategic value in favor of reach.
